History

In 1983, Escuela Secundaria Tecnica Mexico High School was opened in the village of San Roman. It was a gift to the people and government of Belize from the Mexican government, who funded the construction of four buildings. This initiative was in response to the need for making technical and vocational secondary education available in the Corozal District. The school’s motto has always been “Providing Skills, Knowledge and Positive Attitudes.”

Opening

Secundaria Tecnica Mexico started operating on January 10th, 1983. During the week of January 10 to 15, the following was done:

a) The taking of inventory of furniture and equipment

b) The planning of timetables and syllabuses

c) The enrollment of students

d) The planning of a day of orientation

e) The working with records with the accountant, Miss Tun

f) The preparing of the classrooms for the students

g) Meeting with the Managing Committee

The school opened its doors to the students on Monday, 17th January 1983. Sixty-eight (68) students were enrolled in the school. They were divided into three groups. It must be noted that only 50 students should have been originally enrolled. However, the Managing Committee ruled that any student with the minimum qualification be accepted. As a result, 18 more students were enrolled.

The day of orientation started with an assembly. Fr. Messmer was invited to address the students and to lead in the reciting of the National Prayer. Many parents and members of the Managing Committee were present for the assembly. During the morning the students were given their timetables; they toured the school, they got to meet their teachers, and they paid their fees. In the afternoon they played games.

On Tuesday, January 18th, formal classes began. Since then, classes have been held every day.

Mission Statement

Escuela Secundaria Tecnica Mexico will provide quality education, promote knowlegde, and develop skills and positive attitudes in the vocational/technical fields through theoretical and practical applications of learning to prepare productive citizens in society.

Vision

Escuela Secundaria Tecnica Mexico strives to become an educational community integrating learning to produce knowledgeable, highly skilled and responsible citizens for a productive, cooperative, competitive, progressive and self-sustainable society
